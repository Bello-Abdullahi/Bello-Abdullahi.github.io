  <div>
    <h3>Research, Writing, and the PhD Journey</h3>
    <ol>
      <li><a href="https://www.columbia.edu/~drd28/Thesis%20Research.pdf"><strong>Ph.D. Thesis Research: Where Do I Start?</strong></a><br>
        Notes by Don Davis, Columbia University, on how to identify researchable economics topics.
      </li>

      <li><a href="https://sites.nd.edu/esims/courses/research-writing-workshop-spring-2023/"><strong>Ph.D. Research &amp; Writing Workshop</strong></a><br>
        A PhD course by Eric Sims at the University of Notre Dame covering research, writing, the publication process, the job market, and wellbeing.
      </li>

      <li><a href="https://sites.google.com/view/econgradadvice/"><strong>EconGradAdvice</strong></a><br>
        Advice and resources for economics graduate students and prospective PhD applicants.
      </li>

      <li><a href="https://github.com/Alalalalaki/Guide2EconNotes"><strong>Guide to Economics Lecture Notes</strong></a><br>
        A curated list of public economics lecture notes by Koyomi Akaguro.
      </li>
    </ol>

    <h3>Programming, Computational Economics, and Reproducibility</h3>
    <ol>
      <li><a href="https://quantecon.org/lectures/"><strong>QuantEcon Lectures</strong></a><br>
        Open lectures on Python, quantitative economics, and computational methods.
      </li>

      <li><a href="https://github.com/Alalalalaki/Guide2EconRA"><strong>Guide to Efficient RA Computational Work in Economics</strong></a><br>
        Practical guide for RA computational and empirical work.
      </li>

      <li><a href="https://python-programming.quantecon.org/"><strong>Python Programming for Economics and Finance</strong></a><br>
        QuantEcon’s introduction to Python with economics applications.
      </li>

      <li><a href="https://book.the-turing-way.org/reproducible-research/reproducible-research/"><strong>The Turing Way</strong></a><br>
        Guide to reproducible, ethical, and collaborative data science.
      </li>

      <li><a href="https://worldbank.github.io/dime-data-handbook/"><strong>DIME Analytics Data Handbook</strong></a></li>

      <li><a href="https://www.bitss.org/resource-library/"><strong>BITSS Resource Library</strong></a><br>
        Resources for research transparency and reproducibility.
      </li>
    </ol>

    <h3>Macroeconomics, DSGE, and VAR Resources</h3>
    <ol>
      <li><a href="https://sites.google.com/site/pfeiferecon/dynare?authuser=0"><strong>Dynare Resources</strong></a><br>
        Johannes Pfeifer’s collection of Dynare materials.
      </li>

      <li><a href="https://github.com/johannespfeifer/dsge_mod"><strong>DSGE Dynare Replication Files</strong></a><br>
        DSGE model files and replication code.
      </li>

      <li><a href="https://github.com/ambropo/VAR-Toolbox"><strong>VAR Toolbox</strong></a><br>
        MATLAB toolbox for VARs and local projections. See the <a href="https://cdn.jsdelivr.net/gh/ambropo/VAR-Toolbox/VAR_Handbook.pdf">VAR Handbook</a> and <a href="https://github.com/ambropo/VAR-Toolbox/tree/main/Primer">Primer code</a>.
      </li>

      <li><a href="https://github.com/wmutschl/quantitative-macroeconomics"><strong>Quantitative Macroeconomics</strong></a><br>
        Graduate-level course materials by Willi Mutschler.
      </li>

      <li><a href="https://github.com/wmutschl/macroeconometrics"><strong>Macroeconometrics</strong></a><br>
        Course materials with MATLAB implementations.
      </li>

      <li><a href="https://github.com/dkgaraujo/OpenSourcedMacroModels"><strong>Open Sourced Macro Models</strong></a><br>
        Models used by central banks and public-sector institutions.
      </li>
    </ol>
  </div>

  <div>
    <h3>Data Resources</h3>
    <ol>
      <li><a href="https://github.com/Alalalalaki/Econ-Data-Notes"><strong>Econ Data Notes</strong></a><br>
        Links and notes on economics datasets by Koyomi Akaguro.
      </li>
    </ol>

    <h3>LaTeX, Beamer, and Presentation Tools</h3>
    <ol>
      <li><a href="https://www.tcd.ie/Economics/TEP/2014/TEP0214.pdf"><strong>Applied LaTeX for Economists</strong></a><br>
        John C. Frain’s LaTeX guide for social scientists.
      </li>

      <li><a href="https://github.com/ambropo/JambroBeamerTheme"><strong>Jambro Beamer Theme</strong></a><br>
        Beamer theme with handwritten-style annotations.
      </li>

      <li><a href="https://github.com/ElegantLaTeX/ElegantPaper"><strong>ElegantPaper Template</strong></a><br>
        LaTeX template for working papers and notes.
      </li>

      <li><a href="https://paulgp.github.io/beamer_tips.pdf"><strong>Tips + Tricks with Beamer for Economists</strong></a><br>
        Practical Beamer advice by Paul Goldsmith-Pinkham.
      </li>

      <li><a href="https://www.princeton.edu/~reddings/tradephd/public_speaking_for_academic_economists.pdf"><strong>Public Speaking for Academic Economists</strong></a><br>
        Slides by Rachael Meager on giving academic talks.
      </li>
    </ol>

    <h3>Job Market Resources</h3>
    <ol>
      <li><a href="https://sites.google.com/site/pfeiferecon/job-market-resources?authuser=0"><strong>Job Market Resources</strong></a><br>
        Johannes Pfeifer’s compendium of job market resources.
      </li>

      <li><a href="https://www.aeaweb.org/about-aea/committees/cswep/initiatives-and-resources/prof-dev-resources/job-market"><strong>Preparing for the Job Market</strong></a><br>
        AEA resources for graduate students preparing for the economics job market.
      </li>

      <li><a href="https://david-schindler.de/guideejm/"><strong>Guide to the European Job Market</strong></a><br>
        David Schindler’s guide to navigating the European job market.
      </li>
    </ol>

    <h3>Fun Economics Links</h3>

      <li><a href="https://sites.google.com/site/pfeiferecon/fun-economics-links?authuser=0"><strong>Fun Economics Links</strong></a><br>
