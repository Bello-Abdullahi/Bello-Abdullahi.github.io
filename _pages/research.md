## Policy Work

<div class="paper">
  <span class="paper-title">Poverty and Inequality in Nigeria</span>
  <p class="paper-meta">Centre for the Study of the Economies of Africa (CSEA) — 2025</p>
  <div class="paper-links">
    <a href="https://www.cseaafrica.org/postdetails/poverty-and-inequality-in-nigeria?page=18" target="_blank">Link</a>
  </div>
  <details class="abstract">
    <summary>Abstract</summary>
    <p>I examine the underlying drivers of poverty and inequality in Nigeria, where over 133 million people live in multidimensional poverty. I highlight critical issues such as underinvestment in human capital, widespread informal employment, and systemic wage disparities, providing actionable policy recommendations.</p>
  </details>
</div>

<div class="paper">
  <span class="paper-title">Youth Unemployment and Gender Inequality in Africa</span>
  <p class="paper-meta">with Edafe Oluwatosin Deborah — CSEA Policy Brief (2024)</p>
  <details class="abstract">
    <summary>Abstract</summary>
    <p>We address the critical issues of youth unemployment and gender inequality in Africa, emphasizing barriers women face in accessing education and employment. We recommend structural reforms in education and economic diversification to achieve sustainable development goals.</p>
  </details>
</div>

<div class="paper">
  <span class="paper-title">Does increasing the Minimum Wage increase inflation?</span>
  <p class="paper-meta">with Achi Peter Chidebere — CSEA Policy Brief (2024)</p>
  <details class="abstract">
    <summary>Abstract</summary>
    <p>We examine the potential impact of a minimum wage increase in Nigeria, taking into account the current economic state and factors driving inflation. We provide recommendations for a sustainable minimum wage that balances workers' needs with economic feasibility.</p>
  </details>
</div>

## Pre-PhD Publications

<div class="paper">
  <span class="paper-title">Inflation Dynamics and Food Prices in Nigeria</span>
  <p class="paper-meta">with Shehu El-Rasheed and Abdulateef Mustapha — <em>Gusau International Journal of Management and Social Sciences</em> (2023)</p>
  <details class="abstract">
    <summary>Abstract</summary>
    <p>We employ a linear ARDL model to investigate food price drivers in Nigeria. Findings reveal that while higher oil prices exert a short-term positive influence, increased money supply significantly impacts food prices in both the short and long term.</p>

  <span class="paper-title">Revisiting Foreign Direct Investment - Economic Growth Nexus in Nigeria: An ARDL Approach</span>

  <details class="abstract">
    <summary>Abstract</summary>
